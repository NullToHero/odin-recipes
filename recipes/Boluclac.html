Bo Luc Lac (Vietnamese Shaken Beef)

Description

Shaking beef or beef lok lak is a French-inspired Vietnamese dish that consists of beef sauteed with cucumber, lettuce, tomatoes, red onion, pepper, and soy sauce. The beef is cut into small cubes the size of playing dice before being sauteed.

Ingredients

For the Marinade

  • 2 pounds boneless beef sirloin, fillet, or rib eye, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 10 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon Maggi seasoning or soy sauce of preference
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon sea salt
  • ground black pepper to your preference

For the Salad

  • 1 medium red or white onion, cut into paper-thin strips
  • 7 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • ½ teaspoon sea salt
  • ¼ cup rice vinegar or cider vinegar
  • ¼ cup extra virgin olive oil
  • freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 head bibb or Boston lettuce, cored, leaves torn into large pieces
  • 1 cup shaved fennel bulb
  • 1 cup baby arugula
  • ½ cup mint leaves
  • 6 cherry tomatoes, halved, or 1 steak tomato sliced into half slices
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

Steps

For the Marinade

  1. In a bowl, combine beef, garlic, Maggi or soy sauce of choice, sugar, salt, and black pepper to taste. Let stand at room temperature for one hour

For the Salad

  1. In a large serving bowl, combine onion, two minced garlic cloves, sugar, salt, vinegar, and olive oil. Mix well, and season with black pepper to taste. Add lettuce, fennel, arugula, mint and tomatoes. Toss to mix
  2. Preheat a large skillet over high heat, and add vegetable oil. Add remaining five cloves minced garlic. Add half the beef cubes and sear on all sides, shaking pan, until beef is medium rare, about one minute. Reserve, then repeat with remainder of beef
  3. Transfer beef to salad greens and toss, or serve salad and beef separately, dividing among four plates. Sprinkle with freshly ground black pepper. If desired, serve with rice
